Pug Care

Pugs are toy breeds and requires a ton of attention. They're prone to skin and respiratory diseases, therefore a healthy, balance diet is important. They are great shedders wiht highly sensitive skins, so grooming is very important. Last but not least, they're highly emotional creatures which requires alot of love and attention. Make time for pugs!.

This is a picture of Lulu and her peanut butter cake on her 1st birthday!

Pug Care Tips

  • Puppy-proof the home, no matter how old your Pug is.
  • Perform grooming on time.
  • Keep your pug free from harm. Never leave your gate open.
  • Never use a leash and collar. They have a flat face and it interferes their breaking
  • Use proper bedding. Again, due to their breathing problems.
  • Keep your pug on a healthy diet. Always weigh the food before feeding.
  • Do not serve them unfiltered tap water.
  • Use the right bowls.
  • Clean food and water bowl at regular basis.
  • Follow exercise guidelines.
  • Beware of seasonal exercise restrictions. They're a weak breed!
  • Have the right toys.
  • Do not skip vet check-ups.
  • Dental care is important.
